ABOUT

A BIT ABOUT ME

Hi, I'm Chris! Right now, I'm a lawyer, but I'm also studying web development. Where will I go from there? It's hard to say, but I look forward to finding out.

Coding has been an intermittent hobby of mine for many years. I've worked through a number of online courses and tutorials in the past, but rarely had the time to progress beyond the basics. In the past 12 months, I've dedicated myself to coding more seriously, teaching myself HTML, CSS, JavaScript and Ruby using the excellent online resource The Odin Project.

In doing so, I discovered a real passion for coding, and that drove me to take the step of pursuing a more formal education in the field. As of February 2021, I'm undertaking a Diploma of Information Technology with a focus on web development with Coder Academy.

When I'm not coding, I also enjoy learning (human) languages - I speak Mandarin, Japanese and Korean with varying degrees of fluency. I also love to travel, and can't wait to get back to it in a post-COVID world.

TECHNOLOGIES

I am familiar with the following languages and technologies:

  • I am proficient with HTML 5, including the use of semantic tags, ARIA and WCAG compliance.

  • TDD I use tools such as Jest and RSpec to practise test-driven development.

WORK EXPERIENCE

I am currently employed as an Associate in the Banking & Finance team at HopgoodGanim Lawyers. I have been in this role for over four years (commencing as a law graduate in February 2017, and having been admitted as a solicitor in late 2017).

My job includes negotiating and documenting loan and security documentation, as well as providing legal advice to clients, in relation to complex financing transactions.

As yet, I have no professional experience as a developer (though I have completed numerous solo projects in the course of my studies, some of which you can find showcased on my projects page.)

However, as a lawyer, I have substantial experience working in a professional office environment, in a role that requires many of the same logical thinking and problem-solving skills as development.
